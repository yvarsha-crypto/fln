# **Level 48: Foundation Mastery Assessment (Review Assessment)**

**Class:** Review | **Age Group:** -

## **Objective**

Assess students' understanding and mastery of the mathematical concepts covered in Levels 36–47 through a comprehensive review of number sense, operations, measurement, time, fractions, money, and data handling.

---

## **Description**

This assessment evaluates students' overall mathematical proficiency by integrating concepts learned throughout the previous levels. Students solve a variety of problems involving numbers, arithmetic operations, division, fractions, measurement, time, money, and data interpretation. The assessment emphasizes conceptual understanding, problem-solving, and application of mathematical skills in familiar contexts.

---

## **Learning Outcomes**

Students can:

* Demonstrate understanding of numbers up to 1000\.  
* Compare and order numbers.  
* Solve addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division problems.  
* Interpret fractions using visual models.  
* Read clocks and calendars.  
* Apply measurement concepts.  
* Identify and calculate money.  
* Interpret tally charts, pictographs, and bar graphs.  
* Apply mathematical reasoning across multiple concepts.

---

## **Topics Covered**

* Numbers up to 1000  
* Place Value  
* Comparison  
* Ordering  
* Addition  
* Subtraction  
* Multiplication  
* Division  
* Measurement  
* Time & Calendar  
* Fractions  
* Money  
* Data Handling

---

## **Assessment Structure**

### **Section A: Number Sense**

Assess understanding of:

* Place value  
* Comparison  
* Ordering  
* Number sequences

**Example Activities**

* Complete the missing number.  
* Arrange numbers in ascending order.  
* Compare two numbers.

---

### **Section B: Arithmetic Operations**

Assess:

* Addition  
* Subtraction  
* Multiplication  
* Division

**Example Activities**

* Solve vertical addition.  
* Solve subtraction with regrouping.  
* Complete multiplication facts.  
* Share objects equally.

---

### **Section C: Measurement & Time**

Assess:

* Standard units  
* Reading clocks  
* Reading calendars

**Example Activities**

* Read the clock.  
* Read a ruler.  
* Choose the correct unit.  
* Identify a date on the calendar.

---

### **Section D: Fractions & Money**

Assess:

* Fraction recognition  
* Counting money  
* Simple purchases

**Example Activities**

* Identify the shaded fraction.  
* Count the money shown.  
* Calculate simple change.

---

### **Section E: Data Handling**

Assess:

* Tally marks  
* Pictographs  
* Bar graphs

**Example Activities**

* Read a tally chart.  
* Interpret a pictograph.  
* Answer questions from a bar graph.

---

## **Assessment Characteristics**

* Covers all concepts from Levels 36–47.  
* Integrates visual and numerical problem-solving.  
* Uses familiar real-life contexts.  
* Minimizes reading requirements by relying on pictures, symbols, and diagrams.  
* Evaluates conceptual understanding rather than memorization.  
* Serves as the final mastery assessment before progressing to advanced mathematics.
