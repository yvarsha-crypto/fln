# Level 2: Odd One Out

**Class:** Preschool 1 | **Age Group:** Age 3-4

## Concept

Odd One Out (Classification and Differentiation)

## Description

This level develop the ability to identify objects that do not belong to a particular group.

Children observe similarities and differences between objects and classify them based on common attributes such as shape, size, colour, quantity or category.

The difficulty decreases across sub-levels. The harder activities require children to identify differences in quantity or any other characteristics, while easier activities involve obvious differences between objects.

## Objective

Identify the item that does not belong to the group.

## Learning Outcome

By the end of this level, the child will be able to:

Identify similarities and differences among objects.

Classify objects based on common characteristics.

Recognize an item that does not belong to a group.

Develop logical reasoning and observation skills.

Make decisions based on visual information.

## Topics Covered

* Odd One Out
* Classification and Differentiation
* Similarities and Differences
* Attribute-Based Grouping

## Skills Developed

### Cognitive Skills

Classification

Observation

Logical reasoning

Critical thinking

### Visual Skills

Visual discrimination

Pattern recognition

Detail identification

### Foundational Skills

Decision making

Problem solving

Categorization

### Real-Life Connection

Children use classification skills in everyday situations such as:

Finding a toy that does not belong in a set.

Identifying a fruit among vegetables.

Recognizing a different shape among similar shapes.

These activities help children understand similarities, differences, and organization in their environment.
